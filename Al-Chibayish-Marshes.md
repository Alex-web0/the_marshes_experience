# Immortalize Tangible and Intangible Heritage of Al-Chibayish Marshes

## Welcome to the Marshes

Al-Chibayish Marshes in southern Iraq are home to unique culture, wildlife, and traditional lifestyles. For centuries, people have adapted to life in the wetlands, building houses from reeds, herding buffalo, and living in harmony with water.

Our project brings these stories and heritage online, letting you explore the marshes from anywhere in the world!

## Explore Digitally

Our project brings Al-Chibayish Marshes online:

- 3D models of houses, boats, buffalo, and landmarks
- Drone views of the marsh landscape and Martyr’s Monument
- Virtual tours that let you navigate through the waterways and experience life in the marshes
- Interviews and stories from local residents about traditions, food, and daily life

Explore the marshes, culture, and heritage anytime, anywhere!

## Explore Tangible Heritage

### Al-Mudhif – The Guesthouse

Al-Mudhif is a traditional reed guesthouse, constructed by local community members using reeds harvested from the marshes. This process usually takes 4–6 months.

Each Mudhif belongs to a tribal sheikh and serves as a space for hospitality, communal gatherings, decision-making, and conflict resolution.

### Mashhuf – Traditional Boats

Boats are the lifeline of the marshes. The Mashhuf was once the main transport for fishing and moving around the wetlands.

### Clay Oven (Tannur)

The Tannur is a traditional clay oven made by women in the marshes using red clay.

### Martyr’s Monument

The Martyr’s Monument honors those who lost their lives in past conflicts.
